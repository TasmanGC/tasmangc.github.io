Education

I studied a range of topics in undergrad and gravitated towards geoscience. After briefly working in an acquisition company, I realised that I wanted to engage more with the data I worked hard acquiring. As such I returned to complete my master’s to gain experience working on a more substantial project an in-depth investigation of the conductivity of the Woodroffe Thrust.

However the largest impact of my masters was my role in a team submission for the Frank Arnott Award. During this submission I was able to expand on my passion for data under the mentorship of David Pratt and Bruce Fulton. Our team (Team Macquarie) won 3rd Place for our work on Broken Hill using Self Organising Maps.

This submission led to an offer to complete a PhD in the GeoData Algorithms Team at the Centre for Exploration Targeting (CET), UWA. For more info on my PhD consider reading the Projects Page.

PhD (UWA) - Geodata Algorithms Team

During my PhD I was also incredibly fortunate to lead a second team in submitting to the Frank Arnott Award (Team UWA). This time we were able to Place 2nd globally, it was a valuable exprience and I was so lucky to work with a team full of exceptionally capable and motivated people!
