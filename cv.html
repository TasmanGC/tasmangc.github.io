Education

In my undergraduate degree I gravitated towards geoscience. After briefly working in an aquisition company I wanted to engage with the date I worked hard aquiring.

I retured to complete my Masters to gain experience working on a more substantial project, and build a better understanding of the data I was aquiring. I found the field work and data investigation on the Woodroffe Thrust extremely rewarding.

However the largest impact of my masters was a side project generating a submission for the Frank Arnott Award. During this submission was able to expand on my passion for data under the mentorship of David Pratt and Bruce Fulton. After our team won 3rd Place working with Self Organising Maps. This lead to an offer to complete a PhD in the GeoData Algorithims Team at the Center for Exploration Targeting (CET), UWA. For more info on my PhD consider reading the Projects Page.

These survey types are detailed below, and in my time in the field I encountered many challenges and became aware with the challenges of remote work.
